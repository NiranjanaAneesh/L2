Football is a team sport where two teams compete to score a ball into the other team's net. The game is played with a round or oval ball, and players use their feet, heads, or other body parts to propel the ball into the goal. Here are some facts about football: Origin Modern football originated in Britain in the 19th century. Before that, "folk football" games were played in towns and villages with varying rules. Rules The Football Association was formed in 1863 to codify the rules of the game. Players Each team usually has 11 players in different positions on the pitch. Some positions include goalkeeper, right fullback, left fullback, and center back. Popularity Football is played in around 200 countries, with an estimated 250 million players. Benefits Football can help develop a fair mind, team effort, and competitive spirit. It can also be a great way to relax, relieve stress, and keep the body and mind healthy. Longest match The longest football match on record was played in Scotland in 2016 and lasted for 35 hours. It was a charity event for Marie Curie Cancer Care. Name The word "football" may have originated from a variety of games played on foot in medieval Europe. However, it's also commonly believed that the word refers to the action of a foot kicking a ball. American football In the United States, football is called "soccer". Walter Camp is credited with creating the game of modern North American football by altering the rules of rugby.
